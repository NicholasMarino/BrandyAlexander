About

15 years after a comet threatens Earth, an astronomer who played a part in its discovery recounts the story to her niece.

Comet is an original play written by Alan Blake. It was first staged at Chicago Dramatists, as part of Rhinofest 2024. The story centers on fictional members of the Minor Planet Center (MPC), an international organization based in Boston, which acts as the worldwide repository of comets, asteroids, and other natural satellites. Members of the MPC serve an important purpose not only as a scientific body, but as one preoccupied with planetary defense; if an asteroid or comet ever threatens Earth, the Minor Planet Center will be the first to raise the alarm.

Comet envisions a scenario where an event like this occurs, leaving the fictional members of the MPC to grapple with the choice of sharing the news with the world, or concealing it.
